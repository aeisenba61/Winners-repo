Where's Ronald and What's He Up To

Visualizing links between McDonalds locations, health, and poverty

McDonalds has become a ubiquitous part of the American landscape, with over 105 million people eating McDonalds per month. However, research has linked fast food consumption to negative health outcomes such as obesity and diabetes. Low income individuals also have greater availability of fast food, which contribute to their heightened risk for obesity.

County Map

McDonalds locations are plotted against a choropleth map in which darker colors indicate a higher proportion of the county population reporting the variable of interest.

State Map

McDonalds locations are plotted against a choropleth map in which darker colors indicate a higher proportion of the state population reporting the variable of interest.

Scatterplot

A scatterplot with the number of McDonalds per capita on the x-axis and the proportion of the state population reporting the variable of interest on the y-axis.

Sized Donut Multiples

Each state is a donut chart, sized and arranged in descending order by the number of McDonalds per capita. The arc in the donut represents the proportion of the state populating reporting the variable of interest.
